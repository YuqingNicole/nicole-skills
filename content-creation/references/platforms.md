# Platform Formatting Reference

## 公众号

**Length:** 1000–2000 words  
**Register:** High philosophical density. Every paragraph should add something the previous one didn't.  
**Structure:**
- No clickbait headline — the title should be the thesis
- Lead with the sharpest version of your idea, not a warm-up
- Purple highlight for key sentences: `rgb(99,54,131)`
- Centered layout, Optima font
- Images: cold-gray minimalist (Unsplash, search: "minimal abstract dark")

**What works for Nicole's audience:**
- Frameworks she's built from real experience (not rephrased conventional wisdom)
- Contradictions: "Everyone says X, but the data shows Y"
- AI + human intersection — she has unique angles here
- System-level thinking: how things flow, not just what they are

**What doesn't work:**
- Listicles ("5 things about AI")
- Generic productivity advice
- Anything that reads like a translated Medium post

## 小红书

**Length:** 200–400 words  
**Title format:** `[emoji] [hook ≤15 chars]`
- Good: `🧠 为什么努力没用`
- Bad: `分享一下我最近的想法和感悟`

**Body:**
- Short sentences, conversational, no jargon without explanation
- Max 3–4 lines per paragraph before a line break
- No bullet points
- No "干货分享" / "建议收藏" — reads like ad copy

**Ending:**
- 1–2 questions that invite a real answer (not "what do you think?")
- Good: `你有没有遇到过这种情况？做了哪个选择？`

**Tags:** 5–8 tags at end  
Format: `#AI时代 #个人成长 #创业思维 #认知升级 #内容创作`  
Mix: 2 big tags (>1M posts) + 3–4 mid-tail + 1 niche

## X / Twitter

**Format:** 1–3 tweet thread  
**First tweet rule:** Must be able to stand alone. Someone who doesn't click "show thread" should still get the point.

**Hook patterns that work:**
- Counterintuitive claim: "Most people optimize the wrong thing."
- Specific number with surprise: "Reddit karma: 4 to 112 in one day. Here's the actual method."
- Earned opinion: "I've built [X]. The part nobody talks about is..."

**Thread connectors:** No "2/" numbering in prose — let each tweet breathe.  
**Language:** Match the topic. AI/tech → English usually lands better. China-specific → Chinese.  
**No:** Thread-bait ("here's a thread 🧵"), vague teaser first tweets, "just posted X check it out"

## Substack — Nicole's Garden

**URL:** nicolewithlove.substack.com  
**Length:** 600–1500 words  
**Voice:** Like writing to a smart friend who's two steps behind you on this topic.

**Structure:**
- Open with the thing you actually want to say (no preamble)
- "I" is prominent — this is a personal letter, not an essay
- Observations from Nicole's real life > abstract theorizing
- Close with something that leaves the reader changed, not just informed

**Bilingual rules:**
- Chinese leads when the insight is culture-specific
- English leads when the audience is broader
- Bilingual ≠ parallel translation — each version should feel native
- Good bilingual: Chinese main body, English quote or concept woven in naturally

**Nicole's Substack themes:**
- AI era: what changes for humans, not just for tools
- Product + content intersection
- Startup philosophy (not tactics)
- "系统流得顺" — making the system flow, not optimizing individual nodes

**Readers:** Curious, 30-and-under, heavy AI users, want to understand how the world actually works
